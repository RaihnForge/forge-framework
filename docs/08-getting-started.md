# Getting Started

## The Minimum Viable Forge

You do not need software, apps, or technology to start using the Forge Framework. You need **pencil and paper**. Everything else is optional scaling.

---

## Step 1: Define Your Domains

Look at your life (or organization) and ask yourself:

> **"What are the 5-9 aspects of my existence that I must contend with?"**

Write these down on a piece of paper. Label them. Describe briefly what each one means to you.

**For an individual**, this might be:
- Career / Work
- Home / Family
- Health / Wellness
- Creative Pursuits
- Finances
- Spirituality / Community

**For a business**, this might be:
- Administration
- Clients / Sales
- Product / Service Delivery
- Operations / Resources

**The rule:** Stay within 5-9 domains (Miller's Law — your mind needs to hold all of them at once). If you have 10+, consolidate. If you only have 3-4, you're probably grouping too much together.

---

## Step 2: Categorize Your To-Dos

Now look at (or create) a to-do list of **everything** in your life that is currently demanding your attention. All of it. Brain dump.

Take each item and assign it to one of your domains. **Every item should fit into exactly one domain.**

If an item doesn't fit anywhere, refactor your domains so that it can. If many items cluster in one domain and another domain is empty, reconsider whether your domains accurately reflect your life.

This exercise alone — even if you stop here — will give you more clarity than most people ever achieve about where their attention is going.

---

## Step 3: Create Action Items

Your to-do items are now **inputs** (Feed). They need to be organized into **action items** (Organize).

For each item, define at minimum:
- **What** needs to be done
- **When** it should happen
- **Which domain** it belongs to

If it's more complex, expand to the full Action Ticket:
- Action Item #, Who, What, When, Where, How

**Put action items on a calendar.** This is critical. An action item without a date is a wish, not a plan.

---

## Step 4: Set Up Basic Storage

For each domain, create a way to store work. At its simplest:

- **A folder** (physical or digital) per domain
- Inside each folder, a way to separate:
  - **Active work** (Queue) — what you're working on now
  - **Reference material** (Binder) — what you need to access often
  - **Completed work** (Archive) — what's done but worth keeping

This can literally be:
- Half sheets of paper folded over groups of papers
- Manila folders with labels
- A notebook with tabbed sections
- Digital folders on your computer

**You do not need special software.** A filing system that exists on paper is infinitely better than a digital system you never set up.

---

## Step 5: Work the Cycle

Now you have the basics. Work flows like this:

1. **Feed** — Something demands attention (a task, an event, an idea). Capture it.
2. **Organize** — Categorize it into a domain. Turn it into an action item. Put it on the calendar.
3. **React** — Do the work. Track progress however feels natural (even a simple checkbox).
4. **Generate** — Produce the result. Complete the task.
5. **Express** — File the evidence. What did you learn? What should be kept? Where does the result go? (Binder, Project folder, Process folder, or Archive)

Repeat. That's the Forge.

---

## Step 6: Build a Tracker

Your to-do list is a tracker with one field and many records. Expand it.

A basic tracker is a table — you can draw this on paper:

| # | Domain | Task | Who | When | Status | Notes |
|---|--------|------|-----|------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Home | Fix leaking faucet | Me | Mar 3 | Done | Called plumber |
| 2 | Work | Submit proposal | Me | Mar 5 | In progress | Draft done |
| 3 | Career | Update portfolio | Me | Mar 10 | Pending | Need new screenshots |

This is your **Action Log** — the cornerstone of the entire framework. It's a whiteboard for your life. Everything flows from here.

---

## Scaling Up

Once the basics are comfortable, scale at your own pace:

### Low Tech → High Tech Progression

| Level | Tools | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Paper** | Notebook, folders, calendar | The minimum viable Forge. Pencil and paper. |
| **Basic Digital** | Spreadsheet + file folders + calendar app | Action Log becomes a spreadsheet. Folders become Google Drive / iCloud / file system. Calendar becomes Google Calendar / Apple Calendar. |
| **Integrated Digital** | Google Workspace, Notion, etc. | Shared drives by domain. Linked documents. Color-coded calendars per domain. Move here when collaborating with others, or if a digital-native approach is a more comfortable fit. |
| **Professional** | Jira, Asana, Monday.com + cloud storage + calendar | Action items become tickets. Trackers become dashboards. Processes become automated workflows. |

**The principle:** Everything should have the ability to become a physical piece of evidence that could be placed in organized storage in the real world. Digital tools are scaling mechanisms, not replacements for the underlying structure. If your digital tool disappeared tomorrow, could you rebuild on paper? If yes, your system is sound.

### Adding Complexity Gradually

As confidence grows, layer in additional framework elements:

1. **Start with:** Domains + Action Log + Calendar + Basic folders
2. **Add:** Queue/Binder/Archive folder distinction within each domain
3. **Add:** Projects folder for multi-task efforts
4. **Add:** Process folders for recurring operations
5. **Add:** Trackers and Tracker-Logs for monitoring
6. **Add:** Control documents linking everything together
7. **Add:** Naming conventions and date-stamping discipline
8. **Add:** The wisdom principles as active decision filters

---

## The Primary Failure Mode

> **Over-ambition from the start.**

The number one mistake when implementing the Forge Framework is trying to build the entire system at full complexity on day one.

**Let the system grow as your confidence and competencies grow.** This applies to both:
- The **complexity of the FORGE structure** — don't set up 5 levels of nested folders before you've mastered a simple Action Log
- The **tools used to implement it** — don't buy Jira licenses before you've proven the system works on paper

The failure state is **over-complexity before system comfort, confidence, competence, and compliance.**

### The Four C's of Readiness

Before adding complexity, honestly assess:

| C | Question |
|---|----------|
| **Comfort** | Am I comfortable with the current level of the system? Does it feel natural? |
| **Confidence** | Do I trust the system? Am I using it consistently? |
| **Competence** | Am I skilled enough with the current tools to add more without confusion? |
| **Compliance** | Am I actually following the system I've built? Or am I ignoring parts of it? |

If any answer is "no," don't add more. Strengthen what you have. The Forge Framework at its simplest — domains on paper, a to-do list, and a calendar — is more powerful than a complex system that isn't used.

---

## Quick Reference: Your First 30 Minutes

If you want to start right now, here's the fastest path:

1. **5 minutes:** Write down 5-9 life/work domains on paper
2. **10 minutes:** Brain dump every to-do item you can think of. Assign each to a domain.
3. **5 minutes:** Pick the 3 most important action items. Write When and What for each.
4. **5 minutes:** Put those 3 items on your calendar
5. **5 minutes:** Create a folder (physical or digital) for each domain

You now have a working Forge. Everything else is refinement.
