# The FORGE Cycle

## Overview

The FORGE cycle is the operational heartbeat of the framework. Every piece of work — from a quick task to a multi-year initiative — flows through these five phases. The cycle can be triggered proactively (planned concept) or reactively (in response to an event).

Each phase has two dimensions:
1. **Mechanical** — The practical activities and outputs of that phase
2. **Wisdom** — A guiding philosophy or mindset to carry while operating in that phase

```
  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │                                                   │
  ▼                                                   │
FEED ──► ORGANIZE ──► REACT ──► GENERATE ──► EXPRESS ─┘
(Input)   (Plan)     (Execute)  (Output)    (Deliver)
```

## Trigger Types

Before the cycle begins, something initiates it:

- **Proaction (Concept)** — A deliberate, planned initiative. Strategy-driven. "We should do X."
- **Reaction (Event)** — A response to something that happened. Event-driven. "X happened, we need to respond."

Both trigger types feed into the same cycle, but proaction allows for more thoughtful scoping of time, cost, and scope.

---

## Phase 1: FEED (Input)

**What it is:** The intake phase. Information enters the system.

> **Wisdom: "Failure: Key to Success"**
>
> There will be many, many inputs — and they will more often represent corrective measures than green-field opportunities. Failures are stepping stones to success. This mindset reframes every problem, bug, complaint, and setback as valuable input that moves the organization forward. Embrace the volume. Embrace the failures. They are the raw material of progress.

**Input Types:**
- **Strategic** — Long-term plans, vision-driven initiatives, leadership directives
- **Event** — Incidents, requests, market changes, client needs
- **Situational** — Day-to-day operational needs, maintenance, recurring tasks

**Key Activities:**
- Receive and acknowledge the input
- Classify the input type (strategic, event, situational)
- Determine if this is proactive (concept) or reactive (event)
- Initial scoping: Scope, Time, Cost considerations

**Outputs to next phase:**
- A defined need or problem statement
- Initial scope parameters

---

## Phase 2: ORGANIZE (Action Plan)

**What it is:** The planning phase. The input is structured into an actionable plan, typically through meetings and collaborative planning.

> **Wisdom: "Vision + Mission + Values + Market + Industry"**
>
> When considering project constraints or organizing any input, decisions must be tempered by the organization's values, ethics, and mission. These are not abstract wall posters — they are active filters for prioritizing value and effort. Every action item, every scope decision, every resource allocation should pass through the lens of: *Does this align with who we are and where we're going?*

**Components:**

### Action Plan (Meeting)

A structured, deliberate meeting that flows through a complete sequence:

```
Header (Title, Date, Attendees)
        ↓
Agenda (A, B, C...)
        ↓
Discussion
        ↓
┌───────────────────┬──────────────┐
│   Org Ethics      │   People     │  ← Gate: before action items
│   Mission         │   (right     │    are created, filter through
│   Vision          │    person    │    organizational/personal ethics
│   Values          │    for the   │    and assign to the right people
│                   │    job?)     │
└───────────────────┴──────────────┘
        ↓
Action Items (1, 2, 3)
```

The Org Ethics and People blocks are a **deliberate gate** between discussion and action. You don't jump from talking to tasking. You pause and ask: *Are these actions aligned with who we are? Are we putting the right people on them?*

### Action Ticket

Each action item gets documented in a standardized format:

- **Action Item #** — Unique identifier
- **Who** — Responsible person/team
- **What** — Description of the task
- **When** — Target date/timeline
- **Where** — Location, system, or context
- **How** — Approach, method, or process

**Key Activities:**
- Conduct structured planning meeting(s)
- Filter proposed actions through organizational ethics and values
- Assign to the right people, not just available people
- Define action items with full ticket detail
- Prioritize based on mission alignment, not just urgency

**Outputs to next phase:**
- Documented action plan
- Assigned action tickets
- Clear ownership and timelines

---

## Phase 3: REACT (Execution Process)

**What it is:** The execution and monitoring phase. Plans are carried out, tracked, and controlled. Derived from project management philosophy — once the action item is in progress, the job shifts to monitoring and controlling through leadership and sound administrative management.

> **Wisdom: "Leadership (Admin / Manage)"**
>
> Execution is not "set it and forget it." This phase demands active leadership — steering through obstacles, making real-time decisions, and maintaining administrative discipline. The leader's role during React is to monitor progress, control quality, and manage the people and processes doing the work.

**Components:**

### Execution
- **Process** — The defined procedure being followed
- **Action Log** — Running record of actions taken (numbered: 001, 002, 003...)

### Monitor
- **Follow-Up Meetings** — Regular check-ins on progress
- **Trackers** — Detailed progress tracking instruments
- **Reports** — Status and progress reports

### Control
- **Leadership** — Direction and decision-making
- **Policy & Process** — Adherence to organizational standards
- **Administration** — Operational support and governance

**Key Activities:**
- Execute the action plan
- Log all actions in the Action Log
- Monitor progress through trackers and meetings
- Control quality and adherence to standards
- Adjust course as needed

**Outputs to next phase:**
- Completed work
- Action logs and tracker data
- Status reports

---

## Phase 4: GENERATE (Output)

**What it is:** The production phase. Execution produces tangible results.

> **Wisdom: "Walk softly and carry a big stick. Know them by fruits of labor."**
>
> Two principles guide how we handle output:
>
> **"Walk softly and carry a big stick"** (Theodore Roosevelt) — Be firm and fair with all parties. Be reasonable with scope. Carry capability and confidence, but wield them with restraint and respect.
>
> **"Know them by fruits of labor"** (Matthew 7:16) — Everything we produce is a reflection of our character and who we are. Every deliverable, every output, every result tells the world what we stand for. Be mindful of who you are building yourself to be through the work you put into the world.

**Output Types:**

Deliverables, Outcome, Product, and Result are not a hierarchy — they are **contextual synonyms** for the tangible output of this phase. What you call it depends on the scale and nature of the work:

- A multi-year contract generates **deliverables**
- A strategic initiative produces an **outcome**
- A development sprint ships a **product**
- A personal task achieves a **result**

The driving point is the same regardless of language: this phase produces a **clearly defined, tangible output**. The FORGE cycle is fractal — it works for a years-long project or a 5-minute task. The vocabulary flexes to fit.

**Key Activities:**
- Produce the defined output
- Assess outcomes against original objectives
- Document what was created
- Prepare for handoff or communication

**Outputs to next phase:**
- Finalized deliverables
- Documented outcomes and results

---

## Phase 5: EXPRESS (Evidence / External Communication / Export)

**What it is:** The closing and communication phase. Beyond the deliverable itself, there are additional tangibles that must be captured, communicated, and preserved. This phase is closely related to **project closing concepts** in project management.

> **Wisdom: "Who are you? What do you do? How do you do?"**
>
> Express is the phase where the organization tells its story. Every completed cycle is an opportunity to articulate identity, capability, and method — to ourselves, to our teams, to the world. This is more than paperwork; it's the act of defining and refining who you are through what you've done.

**Express Types:**
- **Evidence** — From a compliance standpoint: capture the results, the state of trackers at completion, the audit trail. Know what happened and be able to prove it.
- **External Communication** — Communication to co-workers, collaborators, like-minded people, the public, even dissenters. Share what happened, why it's important, why it should be celebrated, and what can be learned.
- **Export** — Ensure tangible, relevant after-action artifacts. Learn from every experience. Carry forward what was gained so the next cycle starts stronger.

**Express Domains:**
- **Outgoing** — Active communications to stakeholders
- **Compliance** — Regulatory and standards documentation
- **Reports** — Formal status and outcome reports
- **Fundings** — Financial documentation and justification
- **Product** — Product documentation and release

**Key Activities:**
- Document evidence of completion (compliance, audit trail)
- Communicate results externally (celebrate, educate, share)
- Export after-action artifacts (lessons learned, templates, data)
- File compliance documentation
- Archive for future reference
- Feed lessons learned back into the cycle (loop back to FEED)

---

## The Cycle Continues

Express feeds back into Feed. Lessons learned, new requirements discovered during execution, stakeholder feedback, and after-action insights all become new inputs, restarting the cycle. This creates a continuous improvement loop — every cycle makes the next one stronger.

---

## States Summary

The FORGE cycle can also be viewed as five operational states:

| State | FORGE Phase | Core Action | Wisdom Principle |
|-------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Input | **F**eed | Receive information | Failure is the key to success |
| Action | **O**rganize | Plan the response | Temper decisions with values, mission, ethics |
| Execute | **R**eact | Do the work | Lead through monitoring and control |
| Output | **G**enerate | Produce the result | Character is revealed through output |
| Export | **E**xpress | Communicate and document | Define who you are through what you've done |
